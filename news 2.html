Samuel Ortom Dissolves Benue State Executive Council

Ortom Sacks Commissioners, State Exco Governor Samuel Ortom of Benue State on Monday, dissolved the State Executive Council after the valedictory session held at the EXCO Hall of the Benue Peoples House Makurdi, the state capital. The Governor in his speech during the session thanked the council members for their commitment and support in the last four years. “Everything that has a beginning must have an end. It has been a challenging assignment but you supported me and the Deputy Governor to overcome the challenges”, Ortom stated. He also revealed that some of the outgoing council members might be reappointed in the new cabinet which is expected to come on stream in July after the May 29 inauguration for a second term. Speaking further, Gov Ortom appreciated the cordial working relationship with Engr. Benson Abounu, as the Deputy Governor, describing him as a dependable ally. He also commended the selfless efforts of the Secretary to the State Government, Professor Anthony Ijohor, SAN, Head of Service, Mrs. Veronica Onyeke and the Chief of Staff, Hon. Terwase Orbunde. In his comments, Deputy Governor, Engr Abounu described the Governor as a man of humility noting that since he was nominated to serve as his Deputy in 2014, they had not had a quarrel even once. He expressed optimism that the same amicable relationship would be witnessed in the next four years. Furthermore, Daily Post reports some Commissioners and Aides who spoke on behalf of their colleagues thanked Governor Ortom for the opportunity given them to serve in their various capacities.

Why My Military Career Was Hellish – Buhari

President Muhammadu Buhari on Monday described his career in the Nigeria Military as hellish. He made this known during a Special Interview aired by the Nigerian Television Authority (NTA). The president recounted that he lived through the momentous events, particularly in 1966, which witnessed coups, counter-coups and later civil war. Responding to a question: ‘Who is Buhari?’ from the interviewer, Buhari said: “I think I went through hell throughout my career in the military. I was a lieutenant in Lagos during the first coup, January 15, 1966. If you bother about Nigerian history, you read about coup and counter-coup, civil war, coup, counter-coup. I was all in it, including in detention for three and a quarter years. So, I am fully qualified, you know, [to be called] as a suffering Nigerian.” He, however, expressed gratitude to Nigerians for finding him worthy of leadership after been rejected thrice in the presidential elections. Speaking on insecurity, Buhari stated that said he expected a higher level of efficiency from military service chiefs..
